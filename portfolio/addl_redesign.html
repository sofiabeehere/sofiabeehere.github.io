ADDL WEBSITE REDESIGN

(Currently In-Progress - Expected Completion October 2017)

background

ADDL stands for Autism Developmental Disorders Lab, a research collective at the Simon Fraser University Department of Psychology. They conduct studies on child development and provide support for individuals and families overcoming learning disorders like Autism Spectrum Disorders, Down Syndrome, Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorders, and other Developmental Disabilities. To maintain a closer relationship with their growing number of researchers, staff, and study participants, they wanted to redesign their current website (http://autismlab.psyc.sfu.ca/) and branding to properly showcase and connect their scholarly and philanthropic efforts on both a local and global scale.

challenge

This is my first community volunteering project to design an online system around Wordpress, a content management system, since the website had to be usable by the lab manager and its staff after the contract ends. Using Wordpress was decided after consulting the lab manager, current IT consultant, and my partner for this project Bassam Mirghani. The existing website uses Drupal, but the lab expressed their interest in transitioning to Wordpress. In addition, I have to become accustomed to designing around the language and environment of academia, because the needs and activities of a research lab are much different from a business, which is what I am professionally used to.

role

With my partner, I revised the current information architecture (IA) and visualized the initial wireframes for the landing page, contact page, and research pages. I also took part in setting up the Wordpress environment and developing the website itself using HTML, CSS, JavaScript (jQuery), and PHP.

ideation

The project began by conducting an informal interview with the ADDL Director, Dr. Grace Iarocci, and the lab manager, Gisella La Madrid. At the research lab, my partner and I asked them to walk us through the website to point out their likes and dislikes about its aesthetic appearance and functionality. They expressed the following issues during the interview:

  • Too much information on the website, causing navigation issues for both staff and participants
  • Not enough pictures, so the website looks too bland
  • Does not showcase events and conferences they have attended or are hosting in an engaging manner
  • Difficult to track and search for participant forms on back-end side

After hearing their views during the initial interview, my partner and I followed up with a secondary interview to present a proposal, which included colour scheme choices, low-fidelity sketches, as well as preliminary low-fidelity wireframes of different site areas.

execution plans

Once the layout from the wireframes and colour choices were finalized, my partner and I began developing the Wordpress website. At this moment we have laid out the barebones structure of the website according to our wireframes, but due to an increase in academic workload, the project is currently on hold. Thankfully as a lab operating within the university, they understand that students are obligated to put school first. As soon as our academic workloads die down, we plan on finishing the website in October after midterms end.
